## Plain Language

In plain language, this contract asks one question: when AIOS tries too hard
to finish neatly, what useful thing is being hidden? The answer may not be a
new feature. It may be a better way to keep uncertainty visible until the
right MemoryOS, CapabilityOS, GenesisOS, and Hive evidence exists.

## Cross-Domain Frame

Use a city analogy before deciding. A city does not become healthier because
every road points to city hall; it becomes healthier when traffic signs,
markets, legal rituals, emergency routes, and local memory all expose where
movement is blocked. AIOS should treat discomfort the same way: not as noise,
but as traffic evidence that may reveal the next route.

## Assumptions And Negations

Assumption 1: a GenesisOS discomfort branch should become a contract.
Negate it: the branch may be only a memory draft or discovery note.

Assumption 2: the first discomfort is the most important one.
Negate it: the first branch may only reveal that the prompt shape is too
narrow, and a later branch may carry the real need.

Assumption 3: a proposed contract is useful once it exists.
Negate it: a proposed contract can add clutter unless MemoryOS provenance,
CapabilityOS route value, and Hive execution owner are explicit.
